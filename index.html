Personal Information

My name is Ryan Kerrison, I was born and raised in Darwin and just recently moved to Melbourne to study at RMIT. I enjoy playing soccer back in Darwin but have yet to start playing down here. I have a severe addiction to the NBA, with a lot of my free time, spent watching games or just wasting time looking at many obscure statistics of players.  I completed year 12 in 2017, and I spent all of 2018 working at a primary school and saving money for my eventual move to Melbourne.  At the primary school I ran the intervention program for transition/year 1 students who needed extra one on one work to complete the curriculum. I found this work very rewarding and working with children is something that I would like to do in the future.

 

Interest in IT

The main thing that I would say sparked my interest in IT is because of playing computer games. When I was around 12 or 13, I was very interested in loads of different computer games. Always one issue I had with the games was that they never really ran that well on the laptop that I shared with my brother. This led me to saving up all my money and researching information to build my own personal computer. I enjoyed the process of researching the computer components and trying to figure out what would provide me the best bang for my buck.  Eventually after saving up and selling what I could, I ordered the parts and built the computer which worked way better than I could have ever imagined.

 

The reason I chose to study at RMIT is a very silly one at that. Being from Darwin, I wanted to experience the Melbourne CBD as much as possible, so instead of going to Monash or Deakin, RMIT seemed like the perfect place for me. Giving me a lot more opportunities to experience the city, which has turned out to be better than I would have ever thought. I am very happy with my decision to come to RMIT and I couldn’t imagine myself going to any other university.

 

During my studies at RMIT I hope to initially gain a broad range of knowledge of the IT sector so that I can then figure out what area of IT I would want to work in.  With this knowledge I will be able to figure out what career would be best for me and would also provide the most happiness and job satisfaction.
